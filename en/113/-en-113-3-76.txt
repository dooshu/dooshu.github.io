NO. 76: THE APPOINTING POWER OF THE EXECUTIVE 453

This is an excellent feature—This power cannot be exercised by the people at large—It will cause a livelier sense of duty in the Executive—Objection to its being entrusted to the President alone—He may be overruled by Senate—Concurrence of Senate a check on favoritism—Objection that the President may thus control Senate considered—Whole body of Senate cannot be corrupted—Protection of Constitution.
