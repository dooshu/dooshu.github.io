Objection that the general power of taxation would interfere with the State levies considered—Barrier against this danger—Federal sovereignty limited—The only exclusive power of taxation in the federal government is in laying duties on imports—The power of taxation in all other respects concurrent with that of the States—Proof of this—No repugnancy between federal and State power—Concurrent authority the necessary result of a divided sovereignty.
