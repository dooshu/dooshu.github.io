p. 68. the observations ofMontesquieuon the necessity of a contracted territory…: Charles de Secondat, Baron de la Brède et de Montesquieu (1689–1755), was a French jurist and political philosopher. Born of a noble family and trained in law, Montesquieu became a judge in the parliament of Bordeaux in 1714. He acquired sudden celebrity with his Persian Letters (1721), which ripened into enduring fame after the publication of Considerations on the Causes of the Greatness and Decline of the Romans (1734) and the Spirit of the Laws (1748). The latter, a monumental exploration of the various influences on the political life of nations, was especially influential in America during the period of the founding.
