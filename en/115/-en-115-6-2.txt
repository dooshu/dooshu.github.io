II. On Some Current Misconceptions


Before I can enter upon attempting to refute Professor Knight’s assertion, it is necessary to dispose of certain preliminary matters. There are certain ideas which Professor Knight and others seem to associate with the view I hold but which in fact are not relevant to it. I do not want to defend these views but rather to make it quite clear that I regard them as erroneous. Practically all the points to which I now call attention were either implicitly or explicitly contained in that article of mine which Professor Knight attacks.15 As he has chosen to disregard them, it is necessary to set them out in order.
