Federalist 11

p. 81. in relation to operations in theWest Indies: The West Indies are islands in the eastern Caribbean Sea between North and South America. At one time or another Spain, England, France, and Holland all asserted imperial claims to some of the islands.

p. 84. The more southern States furnish in greater abundance certain kinds ofnaval stores…: Naval stores are products such as tar, pitch, turpentine, and rosin that are derived from the resins of living pine or other coniferous trees. These materials were used in the production and maintenance of wooden sailing ships, and were thus of vital commercial and military interest.

p. 85. thateven dogs cease to barkafter having breathed awhile in our atmosphere: The reference is to the three-volume work by the Dutch writer Cornelius de Pauw (1739–1799), titled Recherches philosophiques sur les Americains: Memoires interessants pour servir a l’histoire de l’espece humaine, published in 1770. The so-called degeneracy thesis, which held that animals in the New World were smaller, less vital, and less handsome than those in Europe, and that all living things transplanted from Europe to America would degenerate accordingly, was on the cutting edge of scientific thought in late eighteenth-century Europe. Its originator was George Louis Leclerc, the Count de Buffon (1707–1788), to whom Thomas Jefferson replied in his Notes on the State of Virginia (1785).
