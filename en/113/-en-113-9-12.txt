By the sixteenth and seventeenth centuries, however, the cities of the northern provinces had overtaken those of Flanders in the competition for European and Asian commerce. By the time of the rebellion against Hapsburg rule (1579), Holland had become the leading province of the Netherlands and the territory of Flanders, which had always been vulnerable to the invasion of powerful neighbors, was split between the newly independent north and the Spanish-held south.
