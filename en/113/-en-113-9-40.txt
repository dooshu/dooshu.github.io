Federalist 56

p. 346. Therepresentatives of these eight millionsin the House of Commons…: This estimation of the representative character of the British Parliament is based on the work of James Burgh (1714–1775), a Scottish moral and political writer who was the author of The Dignity of Human Nature and Political Disquisitions. The latter, in three volumes, outlined the program of the radical Whigs (upholding popular rights against the British monarchy) and was highly influential in America during the revolutionary period.
