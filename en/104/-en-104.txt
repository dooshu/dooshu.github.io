Earthsea Cycle by Ursula K. Le Guin









The Earthsea Cycle is a renowned fantasy series by Ursula K. Le Guin, consisting of six novels that explore themes of magic, identity, and balance in a richly imagined archipelago world.
Series Background
The Earthsea Cycle, originally published between 1964 and 2001, includes the following main novels:
A Wizard of Earthsea (1968)
The Tombs of Atuan (1971)
The Farthest Shore (1972)
Tehanu (1990)
Tales from Earthsea (2001)
The Other Wind (2001)
The series is set in a world composed of numerous islands, where magic is an integral part of life. The narrative primarily follows the journey of Ged, a powerful wizard, who grapples with the consequences of his actions and the nature of his identity.
