NO. 29: CONCERNING THE MILITIA 178

Regulation of militia must be confided to federal government to secure uniformity of organization and discipline—Objections that no power is given to federal magistrate to call out posse comitatus, that danger may be apprehended from authority over militia, answered—The project of "Publius" for a militia establishment and its advantages—The appointment of officers of militia by States a sufficient safeguard—Objection as to power to order militia to distant States answered.
