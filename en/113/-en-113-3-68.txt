NO. 68: THE MODE OF ELECTING THE PRESIDENT 410

The only part of the Constitution not condemned by its opponents—It is well guarded—Desirable to have the sense of the people in the choice—Desirable that the choice should be made by competent persons, as in the Electoral College: to avoid tumult and disorder; to avoid intrigue and corruption; to maintain the President independent of all but the people—All these advantages here combined—Choice will seldom fall on one not qualified—The choice of a Vice-President by the people considered and approved.
