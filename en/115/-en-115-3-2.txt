Lecture 2: The Conditions of Equilibrium between the Production of Consumers’ Goods and the Production of Producers’ Goods


The question of how far, and in what manner, an increase of currency tends to increase capital appears to us so very important, as fully to warrant our attempt to explain it. ... It is not the quantity of the circulating medium which produces the effects here described, but the different distribution of it... on every fresh issue of notes... a larger proportion falls into the hands of those who consume and produce, and a smaller proportion into the hands of those who only consume.



T.R. Malthus Edinburgh Review 17 (1811): 363 et seq.
