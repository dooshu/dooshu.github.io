p. 89. Mr. Neckarcomputes the number of these patrols…: Jacques Necker (1732–1804) was a Swiss financier and prime minister of France. Having acquired a large fortune in French banking, he retired early from business and began to pursue public honors. He wrote a eulogy on Colbert (minister of finance under Louis XIV) in 1773 that won the prize of the French Academy. In 1776, he was appointed director of the treasury and, during the following year, director-general of the finances. Bringing order, publicity, and economy to French national finances, he managed to restore public credit, but in the process he acquired many enemies who forced his resignation in 1781. With the convocation of the States-General in 1788, Necker was recalled to national service. His sympathy for the Third Estate (i.e. commoners as distinct from nobles and clergy), however, placed him at odds with the king, who dismissed him in 1789. In response to this measure, the citizens of Paris took up arms and successfully stormed the Bastille. Although the king was then forced to reinstate his minister of finance, Necker proved too conservative for the revolutionaries and resigned for the last time in 1790.

p. 91. excisesin their true signification are too little in unison with the feelings of the people…: An excise is a tax levied upon the manufacture, sale, or consumption of a commodity within a country. In the usual case, the excise is paid first by the maker, wholesaler, or retailer of the product, but then shifted, in the form of a higher final price, to the ultimate consumer.
