NO. 66: OBJECTIONS TO THE POWER OF THE SENATE TO SIT AS A COURT FOR IMPEACHMENTS FURTHER CONSIDERED 399

Objection that it unites legislative and judicial functions—This same provision in constitution of New York which opponents admire—That it unduly accumulates power in the Senate, and tends to the establishment of an aristocracy—That the Senate will judge too leniently officers for whose confirmation it has voted—That the senators may be called upon to try themselves for a corrupt use of the treaty-making power.
